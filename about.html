My Story

From the Dolomites to the World

The Beginning

I was born and raised in a tiny mountain village nestled in the heart of the Dolomites in northern Italy. Growing up surrounded by these majestic peaks, I developed a deep connection with nature from an early age. My childhood was spent exploring forests, climbing hills, and watching the ever-changing light dance across the mountain faces.

My first camera was a gift from my grandfather when I turned twelve - a simple point-and-shoot that I carried everywhere. I documented everything: family gatherings, village festivals, and most importantly, the stunning landscapes that were my playground. Little did I know that this simple gift would shape the course of my life.

"The mountains were my first teachers. They taught me about patience, perspective, and the beauty of impermanence."

Finding My Path

After studying visual arts in Milan, I returned to the mountains with a new perspective and professional equipment. What started as a passion project quickly evolved into a career. I began sharing my work online, and to my surprise, people responded to the unique way I saw and captured the world.

My breakthrough came when National Geographic featured one of my Dolomites series. This recognition opened doors to commercial work with outdoor brands, tourism boards, and publications that valued my authentic connection to wild places.

My Philosophy

At the heart of my work is a belief that every landscape has a story to tell, and our role as visual storytellers is to listen carefully and translate that story with authenticity and respect.

Connection

I believe in forming genuine connections with the places I photograph, spending time to understand their rhythms and moods.

Color

My work is characterized by a vivid color palette that reflects the emotional resonance I feel in each landscape.

Storytelling

Whether through stills or motion, my goal is always to tell a compelling story that transports the viewer.
